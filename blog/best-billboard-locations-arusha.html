Choosing the Right Billboard Location in Arusha: A Local's Guide

Vyoma Billboards · 16 July 2026 · 6 min read

Ask anyone who has bought outdoor media and they will tell you: the artwork matters, the offer matters, but location decides the campaign. The same design that fills a showroom from one corner of Arusha can go completely unnoticed from another. We operate billboards at six of the city's prime points, and we drive past every one of them daily — this is the guide we give our own clients.

Start with your customer's day, not with the board

The right question is never "which billboard is biggest?" It is "where does my customer already pass, and in what state of mind?" A commuter grinding through morning traffic, a shopper walking into a supermarket, and a traveller heading to Kilimanjaro Airport are three different audiences — even when they are the same person at different hours.

Junction and roundabout sites: own the daily commute

Best for: banks, schools, telecoms, restaurants, any brand built on repetition.

Arusha's roundabouts are its metronome — the whole city passes through them, slowly, twice a day. Our two boards at Kijenge Roundabout, where the old Impala Hotel stood, face exactly this traffic: commuters from Old Moshi Road, school runs, safari vehicles heading out at dawn. Slow traffic is the buyer's friend here — a driver stopped at a roundabout gives your board its full three seconds, sometimes thirty.

Retail sites: catch the wallet already open

Best for: FMCG, home goods, restaurants, promotions with a date on them.

Our crossover board at Summit Centre in the TFA complex — home of Shoppers Supermarket — talks to people who are already out spending. This is the shortest distance in advertising: the message lands minutes, not days, before a purchase decision. Retail sites are where "this weekend only" and "new arrival" messages earn their keep.

A junction site builds your name over months. A retail site moves product this week. The best campaigns run one of each.

Corridor sites: ride a growing neighbourhood

Best for: real estate, building materials, healthcare, services setting up where the city is heading.

The Njiro corridor is one of Arusha's fastest-growing residential areas, and its main road carries that growth morning and evening. Our crossover board at the Total petrol station on Njiro Road adds a bonus audience: drivers stopped to refuel, with nothing to look at but the forecourt and your message. The board at Matunda covers the same corridor's retail stretch, outside one of the area's known landmarks.

Highway sites: talk to the whole region

Best for: hotels and lodges, tour operators, national brands, anyone whose customer arrives by road.

Every vehicle moving between Arusha, Kilimanjaro International Airport and Moshi passes Tengeru. That includes tourists at the start of their safari, business travellers, and the entire east-side market town crowd on market days. Highway audiences have longer sightlines and more time — the right place for brand-building at regional scale.

City-centre sites: prestige where decisions are made

Best for: professional services, hospitality, automotive, corporate brands.

Our two boards past Kibo Palace Hotel, in front of the car showroom, sit in the middle of Arusha's business district — hotels, offices, banks. Traffic here moves slowly and the audience skews toward decision-makers. If your customer wears a lanyard or signs purchase orders, this is their daily route.

The kairos question: when to book

Location strategy has a clock attached. Arusha's high seasons — safari months, the festive quarter, back-to-school — are exactly when every advertiser wants the same handful of faces, and a face carries one campaign at a time. The advertisers you see on the city's best corners in December booked them while competitors were still "thinking about it". If your season matters, the time to secure the location is one to two months before it starts, not the week it begins.

Walk the site before you sign — with us

We tell every client the same thing: do not book a billboard from a phone call. Stand under the board. Watch the traffic. Count the seconds a driver actually faces it. We will meet you at any of our six sites and let the location argue for itself — and if it is not the right board for your goal, we will say so and point you to the one that is.
